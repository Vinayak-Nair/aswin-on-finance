Do I need to pay tax in India on money I send from abroad to my family?

No, India does not tax the act of remitting foreign-earned money into India. If you are an NRI sending your UAE salary or income back to India, that remittance itself is not considered income in India and thus not taxable.

For example, if you transfer money from your UAE bank to your Indian NRE account, Indian tax authorities treat it as transfer of your own foreign earnings, not as income generated in India. India taxes NRIs only on income that is "earned or accrued" in India, like rental income from an Indian property, interest on an Indian bank account, capital gains from Indian investments, etc.

Simply put: Your hard-earned dirhams converted to rupees don't get taxed when they hit your Indian account.
