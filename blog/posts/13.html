Why do some mutual funds block US/Canada NRIs because of FATCA?

Many Indian mutual fund platforms or Asset Management Companies don't accept investments from NRIs based in the USA or Canada, whereas no such blanket restriction exists for NRIs in the UAE.

ü§î The Real Reason

The reason boils down to regulatory and compliance burdens, not Indian law per se.

FATCA (Foreign Account Tax Compliance Act) and SEC regulations require reporting of US Persons' accounts. Many Indian AMCs find compliance cumbersome, so they simply chose not to allow direct investments from US-based NRIs.

üìã What Happens in Practice

  • Some fund houses like ICICI Prudential, HDFC, etc., outright restrict US/Canada NRIs
  • A few fund houses do allow it after additional paperwork
  • Platforms like Zerodha Coin currently don't support US/Canada NRI orders

üá¶üá™ Why UAE NRIs Are Fine

For UAE NRIs, none of those extra foreign laws come into play. The UAE does not impose extraterritorial requirements on Indian financial institutions. Indian AMCs only need to collect FATCA/CRS info from you (which is straightforward). You are treated like any other NRI.
