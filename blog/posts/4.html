Is it mandatory to convert my Indian savings account to NRO after becoming NRI?

Yes, it is mandatory. The moment you become an NRI (i.e., you no longer meet the residency conditions in India), you are supposed to inform your bank and convert your existing savings account into an NRO account.

As per RBI's Foreign Exchange Management Act (FEMA) regulations, an NRI cannot hold an ordinary resident savings account. Continuing to operate your old savings account "as is" can attract hefty penalties under FEMA.

If you delay converting, there are risks:

  • The bank might freeze your account if they find out you've become an NRI and didn't convert it.
  • Penalties can apply under FEMA regulations.
  • In practice, the onus is on you to notify the bank.

The process usually involves:

  1. Filling out a form at your bank
  2. Providing proof of your NRI status (e.g. copy of your passport with visa/entry stamps or a certificate of residence abroad)
  3. The bank will convert the account and change the status of your customer profile to NRI

Banks typically ask you to re-designate the account to NRO (they usually keep the same account number, just change its type and status).

After Conversion, Remember To:

  • Banks may ask for updated overseas address + visa/residency proof for redesignation.
  • Update any auto-debits/EMIs linked to the account.
  • Inform employers/tenants where to credit funds.

Don't put this off; it's a one-time formality that keeps you on the right side of regulations.
