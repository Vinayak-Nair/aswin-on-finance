Can I invest in Indian Mutual Funds as an NRI?

Yes, NRIs can generally invest in Indian mutual funds, but some AMCs/platforms restrict US/Canada NRIs due to FATCA compliance and operational risk.

  • NRI KYC update with registrar (KFintech/CAMS)
  • FATCA/CRS declarations
  • NRE or NRO bank account for funding

Funding: Use NRE for repatriable investments (if AMC allows) or NRO for non-repatriable investments.

üåç Country-Specific Issues

  • UAE, Singapore, UK, etc.: Generally no restrictions. Most AMCs accept NRIs from these countries.
  • US/Canada: Many AMCs block investments due to FATCA compliance burden. Check specific AMC policies.

üí° Workarounds

If platforms block you, you may still invest by:

  • Going directly to an AMC that accepts your country
  • Using bank NRI channels

Ensure your NRI KYC is updated with the registrar and your tax residency/TIN is on file.
