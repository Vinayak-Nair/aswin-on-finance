After selling property, how do I take the money abroad?

So you sold a property in India ‚Äì now you might want to take those funds back to the UAE. Repatriating money from India as an NRI is definitely possible, but there are procedures and limits.

üìã Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Deposit proceeds into NRO account. The sale amount goes into your NRO account (you cannot directly credit it into NRE).
  2. Obtain CA certificate (Form 15CB) and file Form 15CA. A chartered accountant will review documents and confirm taxes are paid.
  3. Repatriate through your bank. Submit the CA certificate, Form 15CA acknowledgement, sale deed, and other documents to your bank.

Limit: You can repatriate up to US $1 million per financial year from your NRO account for asset sale proceeds. If it exceeds, spread it over years or seek RBI permission.

üìÑ Documents Required

  • Sale deed of the property
  • Proof of money credit to NRO
  • Form 15CA/15CB
  • Your PAN card
  • Original purchase deed (for cost verification)

üí± Currency Conversion

The bank will convert INR to your desired currency (USD, AED, etc.) at the prevailing rate and wire it to your foreign account. Keep all documents for your records.
