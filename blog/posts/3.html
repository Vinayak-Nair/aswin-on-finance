Can someone explain NRE vs NRO accounts in very simple terms?

When you become an NRI, you'll hear a lot about NRE and NRO accounts. These are two types of bank accounts tailored for NRIs, and they serve different purposes:

üí∞ NRE (Non-Resident External) Account

Think of this as your external money parking spot. You deposit your foreign earnings (e.g. your UAE salary) into an NRE account in India.

  • The money in NRE is held in rupees, but it's fully repatriable ‚Äì both principal and interest can be taken back out of India freely.
  • Interest is tax-free in India. This is a big benefit ‚Äì for example, interest on an NRE fixed deposit is completely tax-exempt.
  • NRE accounts can only be joint with other NRIs (not with a resident family member).
  • Only foreign currency can be deposited (the bank converts it to INR).

You typically use NRE accounts to save or invest your overseas income in India ‚Äì great for tax-free returns and easy repatriation.

üè† NRO (Non-Resident Ordinary) Account

Think of this as your India earnings account. It's primarily meant for income you have within India ‚Äì for instance, rental income from property, pension, dividends or any money that you had in India before, etc.

  • You can also deposit foreign earnings into NRO, but usually one keeps local rupee funds here.
  • Unlike NRE, the interest on NRO accounts is taxable in India (around 30% TDS is deducted on interest).
  • Funds in NRO are partially repatriable ‚Äì you can remit up to USD 1 million per financial year out of NRO after paying applicable taxes.
  • If you have an old savings account in India, it typically gets converted to NRO.
  • NRO accounts can be held jointly with an Indian resident (say, you and your parent) if needed.

üí° Practical Split

Many NRIs maintain both: an NRE for savings/investments of foreign income, and an NRO for handling any India-based cash flows. Just remember, you cannot continue to use a regular resident savings account once you are an NRI ‚Äì you must convert it to NRO or open a new NRO account. This is mandatory by RBI rules.
